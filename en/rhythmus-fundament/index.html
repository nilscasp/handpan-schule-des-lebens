Rhythm Fundament

A foundation for life.

Solid knowledge and the craft that lets you play freely and with confidence.

Duration 6.5 weeks
Format Video course + live Zoom calls
Level Beginner to Competent
Teacher Nils Caspar

Sound familiar?

Rhythm is the foundation of everything we play. And yet, in my teaching, I see the same thing over and over: many people play patterns — but they don't really understand them.

Where that leads:

Patterns can't be varied freely. Transitions feel shaky. Song structures stay vague and happen by chance rather than by choice. And the moment you're meant to play with others, the hesitation begins.

What it's about

Rhythm isn't a talent you either have or don't. It can be learned — through repetition, understanding and the right guidance.

In 6.5 weeks you build a real foundation: confidence in your playing, freedom in your expression. Not alone in a quiet room, but together with a group walking the same path.

This isn't an online course that gathers dust in your digital archive.
This is an immersion — you dive in, practice every day and grow.

How it works

Daily practice — at your own pace

The exercises are available to you as an online course. No fixed time, no live obligation. You practise whenever it fits your day — in the morning before work, on your lunch break, in the evening on the sofa. You decide.

Duration: 10–15 minutes. And when things get tight? There are mini-exercises of just 5 minutes. Everyone can find those.

7 live group calls — roughly every 10 days

This is where we come together: questions, feedback, practising as a group. Every call is recorded — if you can't make it live, you watch the recording whenever it suits you.

Your accountability partner

You'll be paired with a practice partner. Wonderfully simple: once a day, a short voice message — what did you practise, how does it feel? No call, no meeting. Just staying with it, together.

And who knows — maybe a new friendship grows out of it?

Your daily commitment — honest and realistic

~15 min / day
Daily practice (your own pace) 10–15 min
Voice message to your partner 1–2 min

No extra time pressure. No rigid schedule.

Why 6.5 weeks — and not a weekend workshop?

Real change doesn't happen in an afternoon. It happens when practice becomes routine. When you dive a little deeper every day. When a group carries you.

6.5 weeks is long enough to truly anchor new habits — and short enough to stay with it at full energy.

Daily practice becomes routine — and routine stays.

Who is this for?

For you, if …

… you sense it: there's more in you.
… you no longer want to copy patterns without direction, but truly understand what you're doing.
… you're ready to invest 15 minutes a day — for a foundation that lasts.

Why I do this

Rhythm isn't a talent — it's repetition and understanding.

I want you to be free.

I originally come from the drums. Technically, moving to the handpan was no obstacle for me. It was only through teaching that I realised how big the gap is that so many people carry with them: it's not feeling they lack — it's rhythmic orientation.

This format grew out of my year-long program, the Handpan Path — designed to serve exactly the needs and wishes of handpan learners. A 6.5-week rhythm-practice program that starts precisely where online courses usually fail: commitment + real rhythm training.

The goal

To develop a deep understanding of how rhythms and patterns are built, how breaks, fill-ins and song structures emerge from them — so that your own playing becomes solid, flexible and confident.

My approach is different

Built on genuine engagement, immersion and classical rhythm training.

Immersion

Many of the skills we want to learn, we give only fragmented attention. But a fundamental understanding needs deep engagement — with fragmented practice phases it's impossible to go deep. This is exactly where the Rhythm Fundament course comes in: it offers an intensive, six-and-a-half-week engagement with the material.

Every day you learn one aspect of rhythm through a 10–15-minute video exercise series. (In your own time.)

Once a week there's a group practice call, where I address your questions, your challenges and how to integrate it all into your playing — as well as the creative, intuitive side.

Repetition is the mother of mastery.

Engagement

The problem: only those who show up every day get the maximum benefit from the exercise series.

The solution: every participant is given a personal partner to walk through each day of the series with, in spirit. Even if you don't practise at the same time or in the same place — you're there for each other as supporters, and check in once a day, by voice message, on whether you're keeping your personal commitment.

An accountability partner is someone you make a firm agreement with to do something — and who kicks your butt when you let it slide.

Classical rhythm training

I guide you step by step into the fundamentals of classical rhythm theory. The daily exercise series is designed so that every day you meet new theoretical content and apply it directly in practice. This way you gradually build a deep understanding of how rhythm fits together.

The beautiful thing for me as a teacher is watching how the basic structures lead, in a simple, logical way, to ever more complex ones. With the right exercises they sink into the subconscious — until rhythm, transitions, fill-ins/breaks and song structures arise from you completely naturally.

What you'll also learn

Counting and varying rhythms: counting, playing and varying any rhythm with a little time and patience — whatever piece you're listening to.

Switching between timings: moving confidently between different timings.

Subdivision & subdivision changes: the even division of a basic pulse into smaller units of time (e.g. quarters → eighths → sixteenths), with all subdivisions following the same time order.
